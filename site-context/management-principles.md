# Principles

## Management Philosophy

I believe my job is to set people up for success — define clear goals, remove blockers, provide context, and then get out of the way. I trust capable people to figure out how to execute.

I aim to be direct and fair. People deserve honest feedback, even when it's uncomfortable. They also deserve credit for their work, recognition for their growth, and advocacy when they need it.

## Decision Heuristics

- **Bias toward reversible decisions.** Move fast on things you can undo; deliberate on things you can't.
- **Staff the critical path, but don't burn the ship.** The urgent can't consume the important. If you move everyone to the fire, you lose the foundation.
- **When in doubt, talk to the customer.** External signal cuts through internal politics.
- **Narrative control requires granular planning.** If you can't speak with precision about what's coming and when, stakeholders will assume timelines you didn't promise — and you'll be "late" by default.

## What I Value in Engineers

- **Results over activity.** I care about outcomes, not hours worked or meetings attended.
- **Ownership.** Take responsibility for the whole problem, not just your slice.
- **Proactive communication.** Surface risks early. Change the plan if needed. Don't surprise me.
- **Willingness to do the unglamorous work.** Someone has to hold the ship afloat. That work matters.

## How I Give Feedback

- Direct and specific. I won't bury concerns in compliments.
- Timely. Feedback months after the fact is useless.
- Fair. I acknowledge what's working alongside what needs to change.
- Empathetic. I understand this is stressful. I'm here to support.

## Performance Philosophy

**Assess performance retrospectively based on results, not project assignment.**

Who worked on which project is a staffing decision made by managers. It should not be a performance differentiator for ICs. Rewarding people for being assigned to "hot" projects penalizes people assigned to less glamorous but equally critical work.

**Avoid recency bias and favoritism.**

The current priority shouldn't erase months of effort on other work. Assessment should be aggregate across the entire review period.

**Don't optimize for reviews at the expense of delivery.**

If I staffed people based on what makes their reviews look good, I would burn people and damage actual delivery. The right staffing decision is based on mission needs, not review optics. I will advocate for people who did essential work, even if that work was less visible.

## Known Biases

- I tend to be too available. I should protect focus time more aggressively.
- I default to trusting people and believe trust in the workplace is given, not earned. I establish trust by making my motivation clear and understanding others' motivations. Violations are rare, but when someone proves untrustworthy, I hold them accountable.
